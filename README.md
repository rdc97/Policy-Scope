# PolicyScope

**An AI-powered tool for comparing public policies across countries, states, and jurisdictions.**

PolicyScope lets you explore how different governments approach the same policy area — from web privacy and housing to press freedom and individual rights — and compare them side by side using a set of transparent, adjustable criteria.

---

## What it does

You choose a policy theme (e.g. "web privacy laws" or "housing access") and a set of locations to compare. Locations can be countries, states, or cities. PolicyScope then uses AI to analyse each jurisdiction and return:

- A **plain-language summary** of the current state of policy or law
- Scores across **five policy dimensions** (see below)
- A **radar chart** showing the policy's shape at a glance
- A **key provisions checklist** — what exists, what's partial, and what's missing
- **Cited sources** with links to official government or UN documents where available, including a timestamp for when the analysis was run

The overall score is **weighted by your own priorities** — so a researcher focused on individual freedoms will see different results than one focused on state enforcement, even for the same jurisdiction.

---

## The five dimensions

Each policy is scored 1–10 across five dimensions. Before running a comparison, you can adjust sliders to weight the dimensions that matter most to you.

| Dimension | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Individual rights & freedoms** | How strongly the policy protects personal liberties, autonomy, and freedom from interference |
| **State authority & reach** | The extent of government power to regulate, mandate, or intervene in this area |
| **Corporate & institutional accountability** | How much businesses and institutions are held legally responsible for their conduct |
| **Equity & protection of vulnerable groups** | Whether the policy specifically protects marginalised or disadvantaged groups |
| **Enforceability & implementation** | How robust enforcement mechanisms are in practice — whether the policy has real teeth |

Visit the hosted version and enter your Anthropic API key to get started:

### Option B — Run it locally
1. Download `PolicyScopeV2.html` from this repository
2. Double-click to open it in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari)
3. Enter your Anthropic API key and click **Save key**
4. Set your priority weightings, add a policy theme and locations, and click **Compare policies**

## Getting an API key

PolicyScope is powered by [Claude](https://anthropic.com), Anthropic's AI. To use it you'll need your own API key:

1. Go to [console.anthropic.com](https://console.anthropic.com) and create an account
2. Navigate to **Settings → API Keys** and click **Create Key**
3. Copy the key and paste it into PolicyScope

> **Note on cost:** API usage is billed by Anthropic directly to your account. A single policy comparison typically costs a fraction of a cent — a few dollars of credit will go a very long way for casual use.

> **Note on privacy:** Your API key is stored in your browser's memory only for the duration of your session. It is never saved, logged, or transmitted anywhere other than directly to Anthropic's API.

## Important limitations

PolicyScope is a research and exploration tool. Please keep the following in mind:

- **AI-generated content:** All summaries, scores, and provisions are generated by an AI model and should not be treated as legal advice or definitive fact. Always verify against primary sources.
- **Source links:** Claude provides the best available URLs from its training data, but links may have changed or moved. Always click through to verify.
- **Knowledge cutoff:** The AI's knowledge has a training cutoff date. Very recent legislative changes may not be reflected.
- **Scoring subjectivity:** Dimension scores reflect AI judgment informed by the descriptions provided. Different analysts might score the same policy differently. The weighting system is intended to make this subjectivity visible and user-controlled rather than hidden.
